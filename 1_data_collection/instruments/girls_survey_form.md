# Girls' Wellbeing & Football Participation Survey

Peaceball Project – Instrument Documentation

This form documents the full structure, question list, and logic of the girls' wellbeing and football access survey. The tool was designed for girls living in displacement camps in Zakho, many of whom carry the psychological weight of conflict, loss, and prolonged instability.

---

## **1. Purpose of the Tool**

The survey captures four areas essential for understanding the emotional landscape of displaced adolescent girls:

1. **Emotional wellbeing**
2. **Social support and belonging**
3. **Confidence and personal agency**
4. **Access to and experiences with football or physical activity**

This tool is part of a wider mixed-methods study examining how community-based sport can support recovery, coping, and resilience.

### **Section B – Emotional Wellbeing (WHO-5 style)**

Scale:
0 = Never
1 = Rarely
2 = Sometimes
3 = Often
4 = Mostly
5 = Always

Statements:

1. I felt cheerful and in good spirits.
2. I felt calm and relaxed.
3. I felt active and full of energy.
4. I woke up feeling fresh and rested.
5. I felt my daily life was filled with things that interest me.

---

### **Section C - Social Connection**

Scale 0–5 as above.

1. I have friends I can talk to when I'm sad or worried.
2. I feel like I belong to a group or community.
3. There are people in my life who understand me.

---

### **Section D - Confidence**

Scale 0–5 as above.

1. I feel confident in my abilities.
2. I believe I can handle challenges that come my way.
3. I feel proud of myself.

---

### **Section E - Football Access & Participation**

**This section appears only if Section A, Q4 = “Yes”.**

1. Football makes me feel happy. (0–5)
2. Football helps me forget my worries. (0–5)
3. I feel stronger (physically or emotionally) since I started playing (0–5)
4. How many times per week do you play?
5. Would you recommend football to other girls?

---

## **3. Enumerator Notes**

- The form must be read aloud for younger girls.  
- All questions should be answered.
- No identifying information is collected.  
- Consent + assent must be completed before starting the survey.
